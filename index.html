Ferrous Metals Explained

×

What Are Ferrous Metals?

Ferrous metals are metals that contain iron (Fe) as their main component. The name comes from the Latin word "ferrum," which means iron. These metals are magnetic and generally stronger but more prone to rust and corrosion.

Low-Carbon Steel

Contains less than 0.3% carbon. Easy to shape and weld. Common in construction materials like nuts, bolts, and structural beams.

Medium-Carbon Steel

Contains 0.3-0.6% carbon. Stronger than low-carbon steel. Used in automotive parts, gears, and machinery components.

High-Carbon Steel

Contains 0.6-1.0% carbon. Very strong and hard. Used in springs, wire, cutting tools, and blades that need to hold sharp edges.

Tool Steel

Special alloy steel designed for tools. Contains tungsten, molybdenum, or vanadium. Used in cutting tools, dies, and precision instruments.

Cast Iron & Wrought Iron

Cast iron is hard but brittle, used in engine blocks and cookware. Wrought iron is malleable and corrosion-resistant, used in decorative and outdoor applications.

What Are Nonferrous Metals?

Nonferrous metals are metals that do not contain iron as their main component. They are generally lighter, more resistant to corrosion, non-magnetic, and often more valuable than ferrous metals.

Aluminum

Lightweight, corrosion-resistant, and recyclable. Found in cans, containers, automotive parts, and aircraft components. Highly valuable for recycling.

Copper

Excellent conductor of electricity and heat. Used in wiring, plumbing, electronics. One of the most valuable scrap metals due to its conductivity properties.

Lead

Dense, soft metal used in batteries, radiation shielding, and plumbing (older systems). Important to recycle properly due to environmental concerns.

Specialty Metals

Zinc (galvanizing), Nickel (alloys), Tin (coatings), Titanium (aerospace), Manganese (steel production), Beryllium (electronics).

Precious Metals

Silver, gold, and platinum found in electronics, jewelry, and catalytic converters. Extremely valuable and important to recover from electronic waste.
